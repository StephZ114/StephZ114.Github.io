The Powerbrook1000
The PowerBook 1000, released by Apple in 1991, was part of the PowerBook series, which popularized the modern laptop design. It featured a compact, lightweight design with a built-in trackball and a keyboard that was comfortable for typing. The PowerBook 1000 was notable for its innovative use of a color display and for being one of the first laptops to integrate a full set of features, including a powerful processor and a range of software options. Its release marked a significant step in making portable computing accessible and practical.

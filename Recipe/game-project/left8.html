ipodtouch
The 2007 iPod Touch was Apple's first iPod with a touchscreen interface, marking a significant shift in portable media players. It featured a sleek design, a 3.5-inch display, and came in 8GB and 16GB storage options. The device allowed users to play music, watch videos, and browse the web via Wi-Fi, as well as access the iTunes Store. The iPod Touch also introduced a range of apps through the App Store, which launched a year later, further enhancing its functionality. This model set the stage for future iOS devices and popularized the idea of a versatile, pocket-sized multimedia device.
